Takeoff | Migos & Endorsements

Migos

The hip-hop group Migos rose to prominence after the release of the single ‘Versace’.

The song managed to hit number 32 on the Hot R&B Songs chart and also peaked at number 99 on the Billboard Hot 100.

It was also featured in their mixtape ‘Y.R.N.’ After their initial success, the trio released their second mixtape, ‘No Label 2.’ The mixtape was downloaded more than 100,000 times during the first week of release.

More success for the group came in the year 2014 with the release of the single ‘Friday Night’. It managed to peak at number 69 on the Billboard Hot 100.

Enough with releasing mixtapes, Migos released their debut studio album ‘Yung Rich Nation‘ in 2015 under Quality Control.

The album had guest appearances from famous rappers such as Young Thug and Chris Brown. It ranked at number 17 on the Billboard 200 and also came in at number three on the Top Rap Albums chart.

The same year the group released yet another mixtape entitled ‘Back to the Condo’.

Did you know where the dance move dabbing came from?

It was the first song of the mixtape ‘Look at My Dab’, which popularized the dance move worldwide.

Likewise, their collaborative album ‘Streets on Lock 4’ came out in 2015.

Endorsements

The group has been associated with several brands, further garnering popularity and sizable income.

Takeoff | Career

Takeoff grew up in Lawrenceville, Georgia, USA, along with Quavo and Offset. As a kid, they listened to artists such as Outkast, Lil Wayne, and Gucci Mane.

The three would later go on form a hip hop trio Migos in 2008.

Nonetheless, Migos was not the first choice of name for the group.

They initially called themselves Polo Club.

Their first project as a group was a mixtape titled ‘Juug Season’, which was released in 2011, and ‘No Label’ released in 2012.

As time went by, the group garnered considerable success with the release of chart-topping mixtapes and studio albums.

Despite the fact they kept on releasing songs, they did not have a number one record. However, it changed once they released their second studio album, ‘Culture‘, in 2016.

The first single from the album ‘Bad and Boujee’ featuring Lil Uzi Vert hit the number one spot on the Billboard.

It was certified four-times platinum by RIAA. Unfortunately, Takeoff was not featured on the track.
